📖 8 min readBy Fire Fellas Crew💰 Pricing Guide

💡 Important: These are FLAT-RATE quotes (what Fire Fellas charges). Hourly Calgary movers will charge $120-180/hr × 2-4 hours minimum + travel + supplies. Flat-rate is almost always cheaper when math works out.

🧮 What Affects Your Calgary Move Cost

The 7 biggest factors:

  1. Volume of stuff — Not square footage, but actual items. A maximalist 1-bedroom can cost more than a minimalist 2-bedroom.
  2. Distance — Local Calgary moves under 30 min are cheapest. Calgary → Edmonton is roughly 2x cost.
  3. Stairs & elevator — Each flight of stairs adds ~$30-80. No elevator in a condo? Add $50-150.
  4. Time of year — May-September is PEAK in Calgary. Off-season (Nov-Mar) saves 10-20%.
  5. Day of the week — Weekends cost more. Mid-week moves can save $100+.
  6. Specialty items — Piano, hot tub, safe, antiques = extra $100-300 each.
  7. Packing service — Want movers to pack for you? Add $200-600. DIY packing = free.

📉 7 Ways To Lower Your Calgary Move Quote

  1. Move mid-week, mid-month — Avoid the 1st of the month and weekends. Movers in Calgary are slammed those days.
  2. Declutter first — Less stuff = smaller truck = lower price. Sell or donate before you move.
  3. Pack yourself — Saves $200-600 vs full-pack service. Get free boxes from Costco, Walmart, or LCBO.
  4. Disassemble big furniture — IKEA beds, dressers, dining tables. Movers charge for the time it takes.
  5. Reserve parking — In Beltline, Mission, Bridgeland: book a permit so movers don't waste time parking blocks away.
  6. Be done with utilities/cleaning — Don't have movers wait while you finish packing the bathroom.
  7. Get 3 quotes — Always. Prices vary 30-50% between Calgary movers. Just don't pick the cheapest if they don't have reviews.

⚠️ Red Flags in Calgary Movers

  • Hourly quotes with no minimum or maximum — they can drag jobs out.
  • No insurance proof — major Calgary movers MUST have liability insurance.
  • Cash-only / no contract — get it in writing.
  • Massive deposits — never pay more than $50-100 down for a residential move.
  • Truck doesn't say a company name — sometimes called "moving brokers" who hire out and disappear.

🚨 Hidden Fees To Watch For

What CAN vary:

This matters because donation/recycling is FREE (or low-cost) but landfill is expensive ($85+/ton at Calgary's Spyhill Landfill). A good Calgary hauler:

  • Donates working items to Calgary Drop-In Centre, Habitat ReStore, Salvation Army, Goodwill
  • Recycles metal, e-waste, and appliances

This is partly why honest Calgary haulers can charge less — they save on disposal fees.

How To Get Rid Of A Hot Tub In Calgary (4 Options)

📖 5 min readBy Fire Fellas Crew🛁 Hot Tub Removal

So you've got an old hot tub draining your patio space — broken, leaking, or just unused for years. Here are the 4 ways Calgary homeowners can get rid of it, ranked from cheapest to easiest.

1. Sell It On Kijiji / Marketplace (Cheapest, Slowest)

Cost: Free or even +$$
Time: 2 weeks to 3 months
Effort: High

Working hot tubs (or fixable ones) sell on Calgary Kijiji and Facebook Marketplace for $200-$1,500 depending on condition, brand (Arctic Spas, Beachcomber, Jacuzzi), and age. Buyer usually arranges pickup, but you'll need to negotiate, host viewings, and disconnect electrical/plumbing.

💡 Reality check: Calgary's hot tub resale market is slow. Unless yours is <5 years old and works perfectly, expect to GIVE it away. Asking $100 with "buyer must remove" is your fastest sale.

2. Disassemble & Take to Landfill Yourself (DIY)

Cost: ~$60-120 in dump fees + truck rental
Time: 4-8 hours over 1-2 weekends
Effort: Very high

Hot tubs are tough to dismantle. The shell is fiberglass — you'll need a reciprocating saw (Sawzall) and lots of muscle. Plus you'll deal with the cabinet wood, foam insulation, and the heavy steel frame.

Calgary's Spyhill Landfill accepts hot tub pieces — about $85/ton tipping fee. A typical hot tub is 600-1,000 lbs, so $50-100 in disposal.

Truck rental: Home Depot Calgary rents 1-ton trucks at ~$25/hr. U-Haul pickup ~$30 + mileage.

3. Hire a Hot Tub Specialist (Most Expensive)

Cost: $400-$800
Time: 1-2 hours
Effort: Zero

There are Calgary companies that ONLY do hot tub removal. They have the right tools and crews. Cost is high because they're specialized.

4. Hire a Junk Removal Service (Sweet Spot) 🔥

Cost: $249-$499 (Fire Fellas)
Time: 1-2 hours
Effort: Zero

This is the option 90% of Calgary homeowners pick. A general junk removal service like ours handles the whole thing: drain, disconnect, cut down, haul away. You don't lift a thing.

What we do at Fire Fellas:

  1. Arrive with truck, saws, and 2-3 crew
  2. Drain the tub (you can pre-drain to save 15 min)
  3. Disconnect electrical at the breaker (you may want an electrician for the actual wire removal)
  4. Cut down the shell using a Sawzall
  5. Haul to landfill / recycle
  6. Sweep up

🤔 Which Option Should YOU Pick?

  • Hot tub still works AND looks decent? Try Kijiji for 2 weeks. If no buyers → call us.
  • Tub is broken / leaking / 15+ years old? Skip Kijiji. Junk removal is the move.
  • Handy with tools and have a truck? DIY can save $150 but cost you a weekend.
  • Want it gone TODAY? Call a junk hauler. Most Calgary services can be there same-day or next-day.

⚡ Things To Know Before You Schedule Removal

  • You'll need a licensed electrician to disconnect the dedicated 240V circuit safely (most haulers won't touch the panel).
  • Pre-drain to save the crew time (and your final bill).
  • If the tub is on a deck, mention deck access when getting your quote — it changes the price slightly.
  • Sunken / built-in tubs are different — those require deck demolition too, usually $500-1,200 total.

The Ultimate Calgary Moving Checklist (8 Weeks Before → Day Of)

📖 7 min readBy Fire Fellas Crew📋 Checklist

Moving in Calgary doesn't have to be chaos. Here's our week-by-week timeline, refined from 500+ Calgary moves. Save this page. Share it.

📅 8 Weeks Before

  • Get 3 quotes from Calgary movers (us, plus 2 others for comparison)
  • Decide: DIY vs hire movers (read our comparison)
  • If renting: notify landlord (Alberta = 1 month for monthly, 90 days for fixed-term)
  • If buying/selling: confirm closing dates
  • Start a "do not pack" pile — toiletries, charger, work laptop, current week's clothes

📅 6 Weeks Before

  • Book your mover — Peak Calgary season (May-Sep) books out 4-6 weeks ahead
  • Start decluttering: 1 room per weekend. Sell on Kijiji, donate to Goodwill, recycle e-waste
  • Order packing supplies — boxes (Costco, Walmart, U-Haul, free from LCBO/Liquor Depot)
  • Research new neighbourhood: grocery, pharmacy, school zoning

📅 4 Weeks Before

  • Start packing non-essentials: off-season clothes, books, decor, fancy china
  • Label every box: ROOM + CONTENTS + FRAGILE (e.g. "KITCHEN — glasses — FRAGILE")
  • Take photos of all valuables (insurance protection)
  • Schedule utility transfers:
    • ENMAX (electricity/gas) — 5 days notice
    • Direct Energy / TELUS / Shaw (internet/TV)
    • City of Calgary (water/garbage if applicable)
  • Update address: bank, doctor, vet, school, employer
  • Forward mail through Canada Post (canadapost.ca)

📅 2 Weeks Before

  • Confirm movers (text/email reminder)
  • Confirm parking at both addresses (Beltline/Mission/Downtown = call 311 for parking permits)
  • Plan kids/pet logistics for moving day (daycare, sitter, friend)
  • Start eating fridge/freezer items (don't move groceries)
  • Pack room-by-room. Save bathroom + kitchen for last week.

📅 1 Week Before

  • Pack a "first-night box": toilet paper, hand soap, towels, sheets, kettle, snacks, chargers
  • Empty & defrost fridge / freezer (24 hours before)
  • Withdraw cash for movers tip ($20-50 per crew member is standard in Calgary)
  • Test that new place has working: water, gas, electric, heat
  • Take final photos of OLD place's condition (for damage deposit)

📅 Day Before

  • Finish packing — every last thing
  • Charge all devices
  • Pack a small bag of essentials YOU carry: documents, valuables, prescriptions
  • Get 8 hours of sleep. Moving day is exhausting.

📅 MOVING DAY

  • Be on-site 30 min before movers arrive
  • Walk-through with movers: show fragile, heavy, "load last so it's first off" items
  • Keep important documents + valuables WITH YOU — never on the truck
  • Stay reachable by phone for crew questions
  • Final walk-through of old place before locking up (check every closet, under sinks, garage)
  • Tip your movers if they crushed it 🔥

📅 First Week In New Place

  • Unpack ESSENTIALS box first
  • Set up bed + bathroom (sleep + shower = sanity)
  • Test all utilities again
  • Update Alberta driver's license & vehicle registration at any Registry within 90 days
  • Meet your new neighbours (Calgary's friendly)
  • Leave a Google review for your movers 🙏

⚖️ The Hidden Costs of DIY

  • Damaged stuff — One scratched dining table = $500+. One broken TV = $800. Insurance doesn't cover most personal items.
  • Hurt back — Heavy lifting injuries are the #1 reason people regret DIY. Chiro bills add up.
  • Damaged walls/floors — Lose your damage deposit ($1,000-2,000 in Calgary rentals).
  • Friend favours — That's $80 in pizza but $400+ in social IOUs. And finding 4 free friends on a Saturday in Calgary? Good luck.
  • Time lost — 14 hours of your weekend. What's your time worth?

✅ DIY MAKES SENSE WHEN:

  • You have minimal belongings (studio, 1BR)
  • You have multiple strong friends/family who genuinely WANT to help
  • You have a flexible move date (weeknight or weekend)
  • You're moving short distance (under 5km in Calgary)
  • No stairs or elevator issues
  • You're under 35 and your back can take it

✅ HIRE MOVERS WHEN:

  • You have a 2BR+ amount of stuff
  • You have heavy furniture (couches, beds, appliances)
  • Stairs are involved at either address
  • You have valuable / fragile items (antiques, art, electronics)
  • You're moving in a tight time window (1 day to be out)
  • You're over 40 or have any back/joint issues
  • You're moving on the 1st of the month in Calgary (everyone moves then — chaos)
  • Time matters more than money

💡 The Hybrid Option (Best Value)

This is what many Calgarians do:

  1. You pack everything yourself (saves $200-400 vs full-pack service)
  2. You move the small stuff in your car (boxes, lamps, plants)
  3. Hire movers for just the heavy stuff (furniture, appliances, mattresses)

Hybrid cost for a 2BR: $300-450. Best of both worlds.
